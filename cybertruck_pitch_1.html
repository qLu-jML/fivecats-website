Mom & Dad, I Have Something Honest to Ask You

From Nathan, with love and zero pretense — April 2026

The Straight Truth

I'm not going to bury the ask at the bottom or dress this up as something it isn't. You raised me to be honest, so here it is.

As you know, I recently received a palliative care referral. That word changes the math on a lot of things. I'm still here, I'm still building my game, I'm still fighting — but I'm also being realistic about what my road looks like.

I have a bucket list item. It's a Tesla Cybertruck. I've wanted one since the day they were announced. And I'm asking you — honestly and directly — will you buy me one as a gift?

That's the ask. Not a co-sign. Not a loan. A gift, from my parents, to their son, during a time when the things that bring joy matter more than they ever have.

I know this is a big thing to ask. I wouldn't ask if the circumstances were different. But they aren't, and I'd rather have this honest conversation with you than wonder what if.

Why This Matters

I know it's a truck. I know it's expensive. But this isn't really about a vehicle — it's about getting to experience something I've genuinely dreamed about while I still can. Driving it around Waterloo in October. Pulling up to the house with it. The look on people's faces. The feeling of the future sitting in my driveway while I'm still here to enjoy it.

And then there's the part that matters just as much: Kari.

After I'm gone, this truck is hers. She's a hospice nurse driving Iowa roads in every kind of weather — ice, snow, gravel, floods. The Cybertruck is all-wheel drive, stainless steel, and needs almost zero maintenance. No oil changes, no transmission, no gas station stops in a blizzard. Plug it in at home and go.

It would be my way of making sure she has something dependable when I can't be there to help anymore. Something that says: I was still thinking about you.

Three Ways This Could Work

I want to make this as flexible as possible. Pick whichever feels right:

Option 1: Purchase

~$83,435 one-time

Buy it outright. It's mine, and when I'm gone, it's Kari's — free and clear. No monthly payments, no strings attached. The simplest path.

Option 2: Finance

~$1,200 - $1,500/mo

Put a down payment and finance the rest. After I pass, Kari can continue payments, refinance, or sell it and pay off the balance. Cybertrucks hold strong resale value.

Option 3: Lease

~$850 - $1,100/mo

A 36-month lease keeps the commitment shorter and the cost lower. This is the most flexible and lowest-risk option.

After I'm gone, you have two clean choices: return the truck to Tesla and walk away — lease over, done, zero remaining obligation. Or, if Kari wants it, she can keep driving it or you can let her take over the lease. Completely your call, zero pressure either way.

My Business & My Payback Commitment

I want to be clear: I'm asking for this as a gift, not disguising it as an investment. But I also want you to know I'm not idle — I'm building something real, and if it generates money, I intend to pay you back.

Five Cats Studios is my indie game company, developing Smoke & Honey, a cozy beekeeping sim in Godot 4. Core systems are complete. All 8 NPCs are designed and approved. The art pass is underway. Target: alpha by Q4 2026, full launch by late 2027 on Steam.

I have a Kickstarter campaign planned around a $30,000 goal with six reward tiers. The business plan, campaign mockups, and LLC formation documents are all built and ready.

My commitment: If I receive grant funding or meaningful game revenue, I will put that money toward paying you back — partially or in full. That's a real promise, not a pitch.

What Happens After

I've thought about this carefully. I don't want anyone left holding a bag.

If purchased outright: Kari inherits a fully paid-off truck. No payments, no obligations. She drives it or sells it — her choice.

If financed: The truck can be sold to cover the remaining balance, or Kari can continue or refinance payments if she wants to keep it.

If leased: You return the truck to Tesla. Lease over. Done. No remaining cost. Or let Kari keep it if she wants. Either way, you walk away clean.

Kari drives Iowa back roads at all hours, in every season, caring for people at the end of their lives. She deserves a vehicle that won't fail her on a gravel road at 11pm in January after a 14-hour shift. The Cybertruck is that vehicle.

That's the Whole Ask

I'm not trying to be clever about this. I'm your son. I'm sick. And I want to drive a Cybertruck while I still can. I'm asking you to help me do that.

If the answer is yes — in any form, any of the three options — it would mean the world to me. If the answer is no, or not right now, or "let's talk about it," that's completely okay too. I love you regardless, and I know this is a big ask.

Thank you for reading this. Thank you for everything you've always done for me.

Love,
Nathan
